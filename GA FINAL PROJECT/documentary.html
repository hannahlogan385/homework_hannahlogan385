About this project

Cowboy Poetry is a documentary short about the 2017 National Cowboy Poetry Gathing in Elko, Nevada and the poets and musicians who come together each year.

This documentary short opens with a pastoral evocation of an open field at sunset in Lemon Cove, California where fifth-generation California cattleman John Dofflemyer, and his wife Robbin, run a cow operation in the Kawea River watershed of the southern Sierra Nevada. We see a cowboy figure in the distance approaching on horseback to herd the cows grazing in the pasture. This puts into context the lifestyle reflected in the cowboy poetry that will be performed at the gathering by poets and musicians, including Dofflemyer himself. Leading up to the festival I plan to visit at least 3 other musicians and poets at their homes in California, Nevada, and Colorado. Inspired by the films of Les Blank, I am interested in capturing intimate glimpses into the lives of the performers both on and off stage. Taking on the style of a more observational film, there will be no narration so the viewer is able to immerse themselves on their own terms.
