pushout

Pushout

Winner of the Spirit Award and SXSW Director's Fortnight Award

About this project

Teonna, a 14-year old girl, is a freshman at Dunbar high school in Northwest Washington, D.C. She lives with her aunt Ponie and her two younger siblings in the Trinidad neighborhood of Northeast D.C.

When Teonna is unexpectedly suspended for the day, she has to decide what to do with her day. Just 16 percent of female students, African-American girls make up more than one-third of all girls with a school-related arrest. Pushut shows D.C. from a decidedly youthful and female perspective and supports the growing movement to address the policies, practices, and cultural illiteracy that push countless students out of school and into unhealthy, unstable, and often unsafe futures.

Edendale

Edendale

2017 Outfest Premiere

About this project

Osbey, a 65-year old man, has lived in the same apartment in Echo Park, Los Angeles for the past 40 years. Having recently retired from a long career with a telephone company, he is home more often and begins to take notice of his neighbors. Osbey moved to Los Angeles in 1982 when Echo Park was known as Edendale, and it was a bustling gay neighborhood. He moved to Edendale with his lover James, who died in 1987 from complications with AIDS.

Around the fire, Jasper and Osbey connect over Osbey’s story about how he never got to scatter the ashes of his former lover who before dying of AIDS, asked Osbey to scatter his ashes on the top of Mount Hood, Oregon. Osbey couldn’t make it up to the top of Mount Hood when his lover died in 1987 because he too was weakened from illness. Jasper proposes that the two of them go up to Mount Hood to finally scatter the ashes of Osbey's old lover.

Lunchbox

Lunchbox

A Short Film for NOWNESS

About this project

An ecstatic portrait about a mother during her morning routine of making lunches for her family. A tedious process of filling lunch boxes and food bowls for her children and animals who all have individual needs and desires.

Animals and children enter in and out of her world creating a sense of chaos in contrast to her incredibly ordered process. It is through this contrast that it becomes apparent that as hard as she tries, she is not capable of being a reliable caretaker. This discovery is muddled by her maternal capacity to comfort her hurting child, and to build loving and thoughtful lunch boxes for all the beings in her life.
